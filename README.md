# Global Layoffs Analysis (2020–2025)

## Overview

This project analyses global layoffs data to uncover trends across industries, geographies, and company stages.
The objective is to transform raw data into actionable business insights and support data-driven decision-making.


## Problem Statement

Recent years have seen significant workforce reductions across multiple industries worldwide.
This project aims to answer:

* When did layoffs peak?
* Which industries and countries were most affected?
* What factors (funding, company stage) influence layoffs?
* What patterns can inform better workforce planning?

## Key Insights

* Layoffs peaked between **2022–2023**, indicating a major market correction
* The **technology sector** accounted for the largest share of layoffs
* Layoffs were concentrated in a few key economies, particularly the **United States**
* **Late-stage and well-funded companies** contributed significantly to layoffs
* Layoffs occurred in waves, not uniformly over time


## Business Implications

* Rapid scaling during high funding periods can lead to over-hiring
* Economic tightening triggers large-scale workforce restructuring
* Even well-funded companies are vulnerable to market corrections


## Recommendations

* Adopt **sustainable hiring strategies** aligned with long-term demand
* Improve **financial discipline** during high-growth periods
* Use **data-driven workforce planning** to anticipate downturns
* Diversify operations to reduce exposure to market shocks
